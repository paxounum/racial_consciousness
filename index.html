Placing our Race Consciousness

Where does our understanding of race come from? Yes—who, what—but really: where? I wonder if we can geographically place the experiences that have formed our understanding of race. An understanding I believe to be a social construct which deeply informs perceptions of ourselves and our perceptions and interactions with others.

I think that we can. And, I think if we take the time to map these experiences, our deepened spatial awareness might just tell us a little more about the tangents of racial consciousness. Race is a complex narrative which we have been given, one which we also participate in co-creating—for ourselves and others. "Place" is one character in this narrative.

While I'd love to sit down with you and talk about this over coffee, a napkin and pen in hand, unfortunately we can't do that right now. So, instead, I want to walk you through a few concepts so we are talking in the same terms before we delve into some deeper questions. This will be a little bit of reading before we get into your writing. Grab some tea and let's get comfy.

Terms for this Inquiry

I invite you to reflect on the arc of your racial narrative in space and time through three points (or three collections of points):

Focal Point: This is the place which clarified your prototype for what race would mean for you. Perhaps this is where you learned to normalize whiteness, where you were raised into utopic 'race blindness,' or something more radical. This is the place that set your early norms around racial and/or ethnic identity.

Pivot Point: This is the place where you started to question the harmful norms you had been given. It can also be the place where you solidified the helpful norms you had been given. This is the place you had "that experience" which opened your eyes to difference, to privilege, to Power, to supremacy, to division. This might be a vulnerable place, where your knowledges, your behaviors, or even a particular relationship was challenged—where the familiar (your Focal Point) was brought into question.

Point of Return: This is a place or set of places which remain touchstones in your developing racial consciousness, towards anti-racial deconstruction and/or progressive multiracial community development. This place may be one of your own, one personally experienced (a new community, a healing trip, a sacred space), or this may be a place given to you by authors you have read (i.e. Baldwin's Chicago or Paris, bell hooks' Berea or Appalachia, Winona LaDuke's White Earth Reservation or Standing Rock Camp). This is the place you visit to find refreshment, refinement, and/or a call to action towards racial reconciliation. This place reminds you that change is (continually) possible.

I will guide you through a few questions surrounding these ideas. Now, I know these terms are new, but I hope they might also feel familiar. I'm asking you to go on a bit of a journey with me and to narrate as much as necessary.

Story Telling and Information Sharing (What am I signing up for?)

I will be collecting these stories and digitizing them onto an online map. This collection process is part of a technical learning process for me: how to technically make maps, and how to critically ask questions about identity and place. But, I hope that this process is also helpful for you. That said, I do intend to share these stories at the very least with my classmates, and at the most to the "public." I will ask you below your preferred audience, but please keep this in mind with what you share and be creative with renaming yourself or others, if necessary. However, I do intend to keep the geographic locations integral to the answers.

To see another example of a participatory map which I have designed, check out my Pandemic Missed Places Map.

As for your personal storytelling, I hope that my questions can be cohesive yet open-ended, allowing you to answer as you would like, while responding to the idea of the particular "point" in question. When in doubt - say what YOU would like, not what you think I need to document. :)
